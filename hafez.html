Hafez at a glance

Hafez Shirazi

Hafez (born around 1315/1325 CE) is one of the most revered poets in the Persian-speaking world. Known for his mastery of the ghazal (a short, lyrical form of poetry), he expertly wove themes of love, faith, and mysticism into his verses. People hold him near and dear because his poems evoke powerful emotions, offering comfort in grief and joy in celebration. For centuries, his works have transcended class and creed, becoming a cultural staple for personal reflection, gatherings, and special occasions.

Ghazaliyat

Ghazaliyat is a collection of ghazals, a specific type of Persian love poem, written by the renowned poet Hafez Shirazi. Love, spirituality, and the interplay between earthly pleasures and divine longing are at the heart of these ghazals. His verses often use wine and the beloved as symbols of ecstatic union with the divine. Each poem can offer fresh insights into life, faith, and one’s relationship with the universe. This Divan has inspired countless scholars, poets, and seekers worldwide.

Socio-Politicals and all that jazz

Hafez lived in Shiraz, one of the most magnificent and glorious cities in Iran. The smell of naranj (seville oranges) in the streets is nothing short of stupendous. During Hafez's lifetime, political powers frequently shifted among ruling dynasties such as Injuids and Muzaffarids. Under such turbulances, Hafez used subtle metaphors, allegory, and wordplay to comment on hypocrisy and corruption without openly confronting authorities. Shiraz was also vibrant center of Islamic culture, with scholars, theologians, and mystics influencing the city’s intellectual life. Hafez drew upon spiritual concepts to craft poetry that gently critiques societal norms while celebrating inner enlightenment and divine love.
